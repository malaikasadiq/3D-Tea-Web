Tea page

The cup, the box, the steam, the exact brew.

This signature tea page is made for customers who want to understand the blend before they buy it. It shows the leaves, the spice structure, the steeping rhythm, and how the final cup should taste.

Amber Calm begins with whole black tea leaves for depth. Cinnamon bark adds warm sweetness, cardamom creates lift, mint keeps the finish clean, and orange peel brings a small bright edge. The result is layered, aromatic, and steady.

01

Warm the cup

Pour hot water into the cup for 20 seconds, then discard. The warmed porcelain keeps the first infusion round and fragrant.

02

Measure the leaves

Use 2.5 grams per 8 ounces. The blend is loose and visible, so you can see black tea, cinnamon, cardamom, and botanicals before brewing.

03

Steep with patience

Use water just off the boil and steep for 4 minutes. Add milk only after tasting the first clear sip.

Inside the blend

Visible ingredients, clear purpose, balanced aroma.

The blend is not powdered or hidden. Each ingredient earns its place through taste, structure, and the feeling it brings to the cup.

Black tea leaves

Strong body, natural tannin, and a malty base that holds milk and spice without becoming heavy.

Cinnamon bark

Warm sweetness and a polished finish, cut into visible curls for slow release during the steep.

Cardamom pods

Green spice and lift. The pods open into the steam and keep the blend from feeling flat.

Mint leaf

A clean cooling line at the finish, especially helpful when brewed strong or served after food.

Orange peel

Light citrus oil that brightens the aroma and makes the first sip feel more dimensional.
